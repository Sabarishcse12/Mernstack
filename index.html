Quantum Computing is a branch of computing that utilizes principles of QM to perform operations on data.
It leverages the power of superposition and entanglement to process information exponentially faster than traditional computers.
Applications span across cryptography, drug discovery, material science, and financial modeling.

Core Concepts:

  • Qubits – Quantum bits that can exist in multiple states simultaneously.
  • Quantum Entanglement – A phenomenon where qubits are interconnected, enabling faster processing.
  • Quantum Supremacy – The point where quantum computers outperform classical counterparts.

Inspirational Quote:
"Nature isn’t classical, dammit, and if you want to make a simulation of nature, you’d better make it quantum mechanical." – Richard Feynman

Quantum computing concepts originated in the 1980s with pioneers like Feynman and Deutsch.
While early attempts were theoretical, today's models use binary transistors quantum gates and supercooled environments.
